WAVE Project

Evaluation of Waning Vaccine Effectiveness

The COVID-19 pandemic highlighted the critical need for robust methods to assess how vaccine protection changes over time. The WAVE project addresses this gap through rigorous methodological development.

Influenza Vaccine Effectiveness

Bias in Observational Studies

I developed dynamic transmission models to evaluate sources of bias in observational VE studies, particularly the test-negative design. This work identified conditions under which test-negative studies may over- or underestimate true VE, providing guidance for study design and interpretation.

Transmission Blocking

Using maximum likelihood estimation, I developed methods to estimate VE against both:

  • Susceptibility to infection
  • Transmission to others

These methods use household surveillance data to separately estimate these two components of vaccine protection.

Repeat Vaccination Effects

Annual influenza vaccination raises questions about how prior vaccination affects current season protection. Using individual-based stochastic models (morevac), I investigated optimal vaccination strategies in children, accounting for complex exposure histories and antigenic dynamics.
