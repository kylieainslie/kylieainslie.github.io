COVID-19 Pandemic Response

During the COVID-19 pandemic, I contributed to several major initiatives providing real-time epidemiological intelligence:

REACT Study (England)

The REal-time Assessment of Community Transmission (REACT) study was one of the largest community surveillance programmes for SARS-CoV-2. Using repeated cross-sectional surveys of random samples of the population in England, we tracked infection prevalence and detected resurgences before they appeared in routine surveillance data.

European Interventions Analysis

As part of the Imperial College COVID-19 Response Team, we estimated the effects of non-pharmaceutical interventions on COVID-19 transmission across 11 European countries. This work, published in Nature, demonstrated that lockdowns substantially reduced transmission and prevented millions of deaths.

China’s Exit from Lockdown

We developed methods to track the success of China’s exit from strict social distancing measures by analyzing the relationship between mobility data and transmission dynamics.

ESCAPE Project

The ESCAPE (European Surveillance and Control of Antimicrobial Resistant, Pandemic, and Epidemic Diseases) project aims to build European capacity for rapid response to future pandemic threats.

Key objectives:

  • Developing standardised surveillance protocols
  • Building modelling capacity across European institutions
  • Creating frameworks for rapid data sharing during outbreaks
  • Training the next generation of outbreak responders
