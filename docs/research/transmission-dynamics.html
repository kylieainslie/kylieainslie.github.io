Disease Investigations

Epidemiology of Scabies

Scabies is a contagious skin disease caused by the Sarcoptes scabiei mite, affecting over 400 million people annually worldwide. Despite its prevalence, fundamental epidemiological characteristics have remained poorly described.

Using data from Dutch sentinel surveillance (2011-2023), we provided:

  • First published estimates of the mean serial interval of scabies infection
  • Time-varying reproduction numbers showing increasing transmission in recent years
  • Methods applicable to other neglected tropical diseases

Mpox Transmission

Following the 2022 mpox outbreak in non-endemic regions, we investigated transmission characteristics using detailed exposure histories from cases in the Netherlands.

  • Serial interval estimates from identified transmission pairs
  • Evidence of presymptomatic transmission informing isolation guidance
  • Public health recommendations for contact tracing protocols
